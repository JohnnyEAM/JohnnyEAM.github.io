Introduction to Business Management

Here are my 10 weekly journals for Introduction to Business Management!

Week 1

The first week with Cain Kazimogl covered the basis of our module. The lecture began with Cain identifying what challenges businesses face when attempting to operate and manage them. These challenges were Talent, Technology, Globalization, Ethics and Diversity. Through the lecture we covered each one in depth and gained a solid understanding of the challenges businesses face. We also covered hierarchies within businesses, such as a supervisor reporting to a regional manager, who reports to the business CEO who then reports to the board of directors. We also covered the different types of managers within the business world such as Line managers, Staff managers, General Managers and so on.

Week 2

The second week with Cain Kazimogl we covered Macro and Micro environments as well as Pestle analysis. We covered each aspect of PESTLE, Political, Economic, Sociological, Technological, Legal and Environmental. We analyzed each component and how it can be used to gain a business advantage through identifying threats and potential business opportunities. We also covered the advantages and disadvantages of using PESTLE analysis and how to use it most effectively. We then went on to cover innovation, as well as the different types surrounding it. And finally how a company can achieve innovation and the problems which can arise with it.

Week 3

The third week with Cain Kazimogl we covered the international management challenges of globalization, The forms/opportunities of international business, what are multinational corporations and what is culture and how does it relate to global diversity. The international management challenges of globalization are factors such as International company structure, International accounting, and currency rates and so on. As for the forms/opportunities of international business we covered how companies go global, using market entry strategies and direct investment strategies. We also covered the different types of multinational businesses, MNE (Multinational enterprise) and MNC (Multinational Corporation) and Transnational Corporation.

Week 4

This week with Cain Kazimogl we covered entrepreneurship and small business management. Entrepreneurs are Risk-taking individuals who take actions to pursue opportunities and situations others may fail to recognize or may view as problems or threats. We then covered the types of entrepreneurs, such as serial entrepreneurs. We then covered small business. A small business is a company that has 500 or fewer employees, independently operated, and does not dominate its industry. We then covered the different types of business models and why most small businesses fail.

Week 5

This week Cain was not in, and the task given to us was to read over the new assignment brief of writing a 1500 word SWOT essay.

Week 6

This week with Cain Kazimogl we covered many things regarding SWOT analysis, which is a planning tool used by businesses which covers internal strengths and weaknesses and external opportunities and threats. In the lecture today we covered the strategic management process, Competitive advantage, what a swot analysis is and how to conduct it, growth strategies in planning, retrenchment and restructuring and what common mistakes are made in strategic management.

Week 7

This week with Cain Kazimogl we covered the essentials of leadership, this weeks objectives were to Define leadership and explain its foundation in power, vision, and service. Identify key leader behaviors that impact leadership effectiveness. Identify major factors that impact leadership effectiveness. Understand the challenges of personal leadership development. Contingency approaches to Leadership. We covered what leadership actually is, the nature of leadership which is power, we then went on to cover all the different types of power such as personal power and position power. We then covered visionary leadership which is inspired by vision. And also Pragmatic leadership inspired by being efficient in time and resource allocation.

Week 8

This week with Cain Kazimogl we covered E-Businesses. This weeks agenda covered what E-Business is and the difference between E-Businesses and E-Commerce. A brief history on E-Business, advantages and challenges of establishing an E-Business. Components and tools used in e-business and the e-business revenue model. Types of E-Commerce, Data classification, Data security and privacy in E-Business. Types of e-commerce consist of Business to consumer, Business to Business, Consumer to consumer, consumer to business, business to administration, consumer to administration.

Week 9

This week with Cain Kazimogl we covered introduction to accounting and finance. This week we discussed the nature and roles of accounting and finance. The main users of financial information and identify their needs. The differences between financial and management accounting and why an understanding of account is likely to be relevant to your needs. Accounting is concerned with collecting, analysing and communicating financial information. It is key in management because all decisions depend of accounting information.

Week 10

This week with Cain Kazimogl we covered a topic named, adjusting the accounts. The objectives of this topic were to Explain the time period assumption, Explain the growth basis of accounting, Explain why adjusting entries are needed, Identify the major types of adjusting entries, Prepare adjusting entries for prepayments, Prepare adjusting entries for accruals , Straight line and declining balance methods, Describe the nature and purpose of an adjusted trial balance. This lecture was great and will most certainly push me towards understanding accountancy in business and prepare me for my upcoming exam.
